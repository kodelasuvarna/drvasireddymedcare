Journey of Vasireddy

Dr. Vasireddy Narasimha Rao was born in 1956 in the village of Kanneveedu, near Jaggayyapet in Krishna District. He comes from a humble, middle-class family; his father, Venkata Subbaiah, was a farmer, and his mother, Lakshmi Narasamma, was a homemaker.

He completed his education at the local government school in Kanneveedu, where he passed the 10th class with first-class honors in 1972. Encouraged by his father to pursue a profession that would benefit society, Dr. Narasimha Rao embarked on a career in medicine.

He pursued his MBBS degree at Guntur Medical College, graduating in 1982. Following his medical training, Dr. Rao practiced general medicine for several years. From 1992 to 1996, he worked in a government hospital, after which he spent time abroad from 1996 to 2003.

Upon returning to India, Dr. Rao joined NIMS Hospital in Hyderabad, where he worked from 2003 to 2011. During his tenure, he was actively involved in organizing numerous free medical camps across Hyderabad. He also established the Vasireddy Health Care Foundation, dedicated to providing free medical treatment to the underprivileged, elderly, and needy.

Since 2012, Dr. Narasimha Rao has been running his own clinic, Dr. Vasireddy Medcare Clinic, in Nizampet, where he offers affordable medical care. His selfless service has earned him several awards and accolades, including the prestigious Vaidya Ratna award, which was presented to him by the then Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, Sri K. Rosaiah garu. Now he is practising as a general physician and Diabetologist in Nizampet.
